Our Roads

Our roads here in Aotearoa may not always be the best quality surface to drive on, but they'll usually make up for what they lack in that department with the sights you see as you drive. Going up the Kaimai's, you get to see a view, so much so that there is a place to stop and see the view properly on the way up. The road from Christchurch to Greymouth will lead you through Arthur's Pass, a wonderful township popular for exploring the National Park. That road isn't very fun with many corners, steep mountains and more one way bridges than possibly needed, but you get good views to make up for it on the way.

Rotorua

Located in the Bay of Plenty, Rotorua is one of the best countries in the country for scenic views. The iconic (and infamous) smell is that of sulphur from the geysers. The most notable geyser is the Pohutu geyser at Whakarewarewa. The Lake Rotorua lies here too, which is how the town got its name. Rotorua also offers hot mud pools and great geothermic activity which is sourced from the Rotorua Caldera, where the town lies. There is also a good amount of Maori culture present in Rotorua, so if that's something you want to learn more about, then Rotorua is a good place to visit.

Tokoroa Talking Poles

Tokoroa is the home of the Talking Poles. It is the largest settlement in the South Waikato District and is only 30km from Rotorua. The poles were an idea from 1996 for the retail area and the residents supported it. 46 poles are on display at various places around the town, like in playgrounds, on the side of the road, in roundabouts, and the most notable one, the big chainsaw man standing near town centre. These poles aim to depict the large varity of cultures in the community. Tokoroa is a town that stems from a forestry background, so most of these poles are primarily made from pine and other woods, though such materials like ceramic and steel are also used. Tokoroa also has a lake - Lake Moananui. It is a small lake that only take half an hour to walk around, and it's a relaxing little walk.

Tirau Sheep & Dog buildings

Driving through Tirau, going either North or South, you will see the huge sheep and dog buildings. These large buildings are made of corregated iron, and are a popular photo taking point for those travelling through the town for the first (or however many) time. Tirau is - like Tokoroa - part of the South Waikato, which is just a smaller sub divison of the Waikato. Tirau is 50km South of Hamilton - Waikato's city - and 31km from Tokoroa. One of these large animal buildings is the information centre for the town.
